The Constitution of India guarantees the Right to Equality under Article 14, which ensures equal treatment under the law for all citizens.

The Right to Education is a fundamental right under Article 21A, mandating free and compulsory education for children aged 6 to 14 years.

The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 safeguards consumer rights including the right to safety, information, choice, and redressal.

Under the POSH Act, 2013, employers must set up an Internal Complaints Committee to handle cases of sexual harassment at the workplace.

The Right to Information Act, 2005 allows citizens to access information from public authorities, promoting transparency in governance.

The Indian Contract Act, 1872 defines and regulates contracts, including offer, acceptance, consideration, and breach of contract.

The Rent Control Act protects tenants from unfair eviction and excessive rent by regulating the landlord-tenant relationship.

The Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 bans the giving or taking of dowry in marriages and punishes those who demand or accept dowry.

The Indian Penal Code (IPC), 1860 is the main criminal code of India and defines various crimes and their punishments.

The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012 provides a legal framework to protect children from sexual abuse.

The Domestic Violence Act, 2005 protects women from abuse in domestic relationships, including physical, emotional, and financial abuse.

The Maternity Benefit Act, 1961 provides maternity leave and other benefits to pregnant and nursing women employed in certain sectors.

The Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987 provides free legal aid to eligible citizens and promotes legal awareness.

The Indian Evidence Act, 1872 outlines the rules relating to admissibility of evidence in Indian courts.

The Juvenile Justice Act deals with children in conflict with the law and provides for rehabilitation and care of children in need.

The Indian Succession Act governs inheritance and succession of property when a person dies without a will.

The Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 governs marriage, divorce, and maintenance among Hindus in India.

The Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 regulates all aspects of road transport and mandates compensation for road accident victims.

The Environment Protection Act, 1986 empowers the government to take measures to protect and improve environmental quality.

The Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 prevents atrocities against SC/ST communities and provides for special courts for their trial.
